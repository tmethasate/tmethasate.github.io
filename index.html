About

My work sits between theoretical econometrics and empirical finance, with a particular focus on how modern statistical tools can better handle noisy market data.

I am a current Certificate of Postgraduate Study (CPGS) student in the Faculty of Economics at the University of Cambridge (1st year PhD). My research interests cluster on econometrics theory, high-frequency financial econometrics, and empirical finance.

Research Interests

The core themes below shape both the technical and applied side of my research agenda.

Econometrics Theory

Developing inference and estimation tools for data environments where standard assumptions break down.

High-Frequency Financial Econometrics

Studying the statistical structure of transaction-level data, market frictions, and microstructure noise.

Empirical Finance

Connecting theoretical econometric methods to substantive questions in asset prices and financial markets.

Current project

Estimating Cross-Covariance of Microstructure Noises

I am developing an estimator for the cross-covariance of microstructure noises in high-frequency financial data in a general setting. The estimator is an application of the ReMeDI framework proposed by Li and Linton (2022).

Research lens

Measurement error, dependence structure, and reliable estimation under market microstructure frictions.
